International Traffic in Arms Regulations - US Defense Export Control Requirements

What is ITAR?

The International Traffic in Arms Regulations (ITAR) is a United States regulatory framework that controls the export and import of defense-related articles, services, and technical data. ITAR is administered by the U.S. Department of State's Directorate of Defense Trade Controls (DDTC) and is designed to safeguard U.S. national security interests by preventing sensitive defense technologies from being accessed by foreign entities without proper authorization.

Key Facts About ITAR

  • Governing Body: U.S. Department of State, Directorate of Defense Trade Controls (DDTC)
  • Primary Region: United States (with global implications)
  • Established: 1976, with regular updates
  • Legal Authority: Arms Export Control Act (AECA)
  • Registration Requirement: Annual registration with DDTC required for manufacturers, exporters, and brokers of defense articles

ITAR Compliance Requirements

Organizations that manufacture, export, or temporarily import defense articles, furnish defense services, or transfer technical data must comply with ITAR. The following table outlines the key requirements:

Requirement Category Description Implementation Considerations
Registration Organizations that manufacture, export, or broker defense articles or services must register with DDTC Annual registration fee based on company size; must be renewed yearly
USML Classification Determine if products/services fall under the U.S. Munitions List (USML) categories Requires thorough product analysis and possibly commodity jurisdiction requests
Export Licensing Obtain appropriate licenses before exporting defense articles or technical data Different license types available (DSP-5, DSP-73, DSP-61); processing time varies
Technical Data Control Protect technical data related to defense articles from unauthorized foreign access Includes blueprints, drawings, photographs, plans, instructions, software
Access Controls Restrict physical and electronic access to ITAR-controlled data May require segregated networks, secure facilities, and citizenship verification
Employee Screening Verify U.S. citizenship or appropriate authorization for employees accessing ITAR data Includes background checks and ongoing verification processes
Record Keeping Maintain detailed records of all ITAR-related activities for at least 5 years Includes export licenses, technical assistance agreements, and shipping documentation
Compliance Program Establish a formal ITAR compliance program with policies and procedures Should include training, auditing, and incident response procedures
Supply Chain Management Ensure suppliers and subcontractors comply with ITAR requirements Requires flow-down clauses in contracts and supplier audits
Violation Reporting Self-disclose any ITAR violations to DDTC Voluntary disclosure may mitigate penalties if properly handled

Implementation Steps for ITAR Compliance

Achieving ITAR compliance requires a systematic approach. Follow these steps to implement an effective ITAR compliance program:

Determine Applicability

Assess whether your organization manufactures, exports, or handles defense articles, services, or technical data that fall under ITAR jurisdiction. Review the U.S. Munitions List (USML) to identify if your products or services are covered.

Register with DDTC

If ITAR applies to your organization, register with the Directorate of Defense Trade Controls through the Defense Export Control and Compliance System (DECCS). Registration must be renewed annually and requires payment of a fee based on your organization's size.

Develop an ITAR Compliance Program

Create a comprehensive compliance program that includes policies, procedures, and controls to ensure ITAR requirements are met. This should cover classification, licensing, technical data protection, and record-keeping.

Implement Access Controls

Establish physical and electronic access controls to prevent unauthorized foreign nationals from accessing ITAR-controlled technical data. This may include secure facilities, segregated networks, and data encryption.

Screen Personnel

Implement procedures to verify the citizenship or authorization status of employees who will have access to ITAR-controlled items or data. Document this verification process and maintain records.

Establish License Management

Create processes for obtaining and managing export licenses, technical assistance agreements (TAAs), and manufacturing license agreements (MLAs) as required for your specific activities.

Train Employees

Provide comprehensive training to all employees involved with ITAR-controlled items or data. Training should cover ITAR requirements, company policies, and individual responsibilities.

Manage Supply Chain

Ensure that suppliers and subcontractors handling ITAR-controlled items understand and comply with ITAR requirements. Include appropriate clauses in contracts and agreements.

Implement Record-Keeping

Establish systems to maintain all required records for at least five years, including export licenses, technical assistance agreements, and shipping documentation.

Conduct Regular Audits

Perform internal audits of your ITAR compliance program to identify and address any gaps or violations. Consider periodic third-party audits for additional assurance.

ITAR Compliance for IT Products

For IT products and services, ITAR compliance presents unique challenges, particularly regarding technical data protection and cloud services. Key considerations include:

IT-Specific ITAR Requirements

  • Data Segregation: ITAR-controlled technical data must be physically and logically separated from non-ITAR data
  • Cloud Services: Cloud environments hosting ITAR data must ensure all infrastructure is located in the U.S. and accessed only by U.S. persons
  • Encryption: Strong encryption must be implemented for data at rest and in transit
  • Access Controls: Multi-factor authentication and strict role-based access controls should be implemented
  • Monitoring: Continuous monitoring for unauthorized access
