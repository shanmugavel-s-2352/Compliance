DEFSTAN

UK Defence Standards

What are Defence Standards (DEFSTAN)?

Defence Standards, commonly known as DEFSTAN, are a series of standards developed by the UK Ministry of Defence (MOD) to help standardize equipment and processes used by the UK Armed Forces. These standards cover a wide range of areas, including cybersecurity, product safety, quality management, and technical specifications for defence equipment and services.

Key Facts About DEFSTAN

  • Governing Body: UK Ministry of Defence
  • Primary Region: United Kingdom
  • Legal Authority: Contractual requirements for UK defence procurement
  • Compliance Requirement: Mandatory for suppliers to the UK MOD and defence sector
  • Focus Areas: Cybersecurity, product security, quality assurance, technical specifications

Key DEFSTAN Standards for Cybersecurity

Several Defence Standards are particularly relevant for cybersecurity and IT product compliance:

Standard Title Description
DEFSTAN 05-138 Cyber Security for Defence Suppliers Defines cybersecurity requirements for defence suppliers, aligned with the UK's Cyber Assessment Framework (CAF)
DEFSTAN 00-055 Requirements for Security of Electronic Data Systems Specifies security requirements for electronic data systems handling classified information
DEFSTAN 05-135 Cyber Security for Military Systems Provides requirements for cybersecurity in military systems and equipment
DEFSTAN 23-009 Generic Vehicle Architecture (GVA) Includes security requirements for vehicle electronic systems
DEFSTAN 00-056 Safety Management Requirements for Defence Systems Includes security considerations for safety-critical systems
