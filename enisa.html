ENISA

European Union Agency for Cybersecurity

What is ENISA?

The European Union Agency for Cybersecurity (ENISA) is the EU's agency dedicated to achieving a high common level of cybersecurity across Europe. Established in 2004, ENISA works closely with Member States and the private sector to deliver advice and solutions as well as improving the trustworthiness of ICT products, services, and processes through cybersecurity certification schemes.

Key Facts About ENISA

  • Governing Body: European Union
  • Primary Region: European Union Member States
  • Legal Authority: Regulation (EU) 2019/881 (Cybersecurity Act)
  • Compliance Requirement: Varies by scheme; some voluntary, some mandatory
  • Focus Areas: Cybersecurity certification, critical infrastructure protection, incident response, cybersecurity exercises, and awareness

ENISA's Role in EU Cybersecurity

ENISA plays several key roles in the European cybersecurity landscape:

Role Description
Expertise Provides independent advice, analysis, and information on cybersecurity
Certification Develops and maintains the European Cybersecurity Certification Framework
Operational Cooperation Supports operational cooperation among Member States, EU institutions, and international stakeholders
Capacity Building Assists Member States and EU institutions in strengthening cybersecurity capabilities
Awareness Raising Enhances awareness of cybersecurity issues for citizens and organizations
Market Monitoring Analyzes trends in the cybersecurity market to better address EU needs
